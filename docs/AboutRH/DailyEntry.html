There is a big difference between "likely" and "more likely". Lets say you have a scale ranging       
from 0% to 100%, and the you point at whatever is being measured at 50%. Lets say the scale is at 
10%. Since 10%<50%, this scale would be considered unlikely. But now lets say it increases by 10%.  
Its MORE likely, but still unlikely. How to people not understand this?
